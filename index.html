The 5th China Summer Workshop on Information Management (CSWIM 2011) will be held in Harbin, China on June 25-26th, 2011. It will be hosted by the School of Management, Harbin Institute of Technology. The purpose of the workshop is to create a bridge to promote lively exchanges and collaborations between IS scholars in China and other countries, and to study how information systems and technologies affect individuals, businesses, organizations, and societies.

Harbin, also known as the "Oriental Paris", "Oriental Moscow", and the "Ice City", is the capital city of Heilongjiang Province in Northeast China. It lies on the southern bank of the Songhua River. Harbin is the tenth largest city in China, serving as a key political, economic, scientific, cultural and communications hub in Northeastern China.

We welcome submissions of original research papers addressing issues concerning the theory, design, development, evaluation, and application of information systems and management. Since it is a research workshop, CSWIM encourages submissions of research-in-progress that are innovative and thought-provoking. Research articles particularly sought after are those driven by real-world business problems and validated with proper research methodologies.

For a list of topics of interest and submission instructions, see Call for Papers (PDF).
